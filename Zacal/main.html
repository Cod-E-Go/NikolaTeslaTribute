Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla, circa 1890

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Tesla's patents and theoretical work helped lay the foundation for wireless communication and radio. His alternating current (AC) induction motor is considered one of the ten greatest discoveries of all time.

Born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Croatia, Tesla emigrated to the United States in 1884 and worked with Thomas Edison before becoming a rival with his promotion of alternating current over Edison's direct current.

Tesla's other notable inventions include the Tesla coil, a device that produces high-voltage, high-frequency alternating-current electricity, and the Tesla turbine.

Despite facing financial difficulties and being largely unrecognized during his lifetime, Tesla's work has had a profound impact on the world, and he is now celebrated as one of the greatest inventors in history.
